Section I

Historical Context

The origin of the Vietnam War and the draft system

The Vietnam Conflict until Gulf of Tonkin

Vietnam had been in a state of conflict with many countries attempting to take control. This took a major turn when communist and national leader Ho Chi Minh decided Vietnam was to get its own independence. He defeated French forces in the battle of Dien Bien Phu in 1954 which set the stage for the Geneva Accords to establish a Communist north and southern Republic under Emperor Bao Dai.

With Cold War tensions rising and an effort to maintain a containment policy, a newly communist vietnam threatened American interests. The Gulf of Tonkin incident in 1964 became the official cause for American military escalation, and not so long after the nation was unofficially at war.

The lottery

The Selective Service lottery was enforced based on birth date priority. It involved small capsules that represented every day of the year. These capsules were drawn from a jar at random and whichever date came up first was assigned the highest deployment priority. This was found to be bias towards later birth months.

College enrollment, certain occupations, statuses related to hardships, and medical conditions were reasons to be deferred. Those without access to lawyers, doctors, or universities had no such options which was problematic, especially for minority communities.

Photograph of the December 1, 1969 draft lottery drawing, showing the blue plastic capsules being "randomized" by military officials.

Why this context matters

The draft was built based on a Cold War foreign policy that required compulsory service. This was paired with the Selective Service system which had been sending American men to war since World War I. What made Vietnam different was not the existence of the draft but rather the combination of an undeclared war, a clearly unfair lottery, and a generation of New Left individuals advocating for social equality.

The Vietnam War draft is one of the most consequential reforms in modern American history. It ended one system, compulsory military service, and produced another, the all-volunteer force, that has been in effect for every American conflict since.
