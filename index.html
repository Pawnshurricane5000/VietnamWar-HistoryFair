Drafted Into Dissent

Era 1964 – 1973
Research Question

To what extent did public resistance to the Vietnam War draft, particularly opposition to student, class-based, and racial exemptions, transform America's system of military conscription and contribute to the shift toward an all-volunteer force?

Thesis Statement

Public resistance against the Vietnam War Draft transformed the conscription system in America by greatly contributing to the shift toward an all-volunteer force since it created widespread civil disobedience and draft refusal making conscription difficult to enforce, and the racial and class inequalities within the draft were exposed to the public which extended support of the movement to Civil Rights Organizations.

Why does this topic matter?

One of the most significant changes in American history is the reform of the Vietnam War draft, which led to a complete transformation of conscription into an all-volunteer army system. However, the path leading to that change resulted in violence due to the growing discontent the citizens of America displayed.

During the period from 1960 to 1973, about 2.5 million men were drafted. Hundreds of thousands deserted and more than a million people evaded the draft one way or another. A system that worked for decades during World War I and World War II failed during that decade. This happened because the American society decided not to cooperate any longer with the class inequality the draft demonstrated as well as the amount of innocent lives that were being taken away for a war that did not concern the US government.
